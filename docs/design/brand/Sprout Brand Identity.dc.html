SPROUT // working name
BRAND IDENTITY · v1

Quiet care for living things.

The personality, voice and visual identity for a soil-watering system that treats every plant like it's alive — ready to share, publish, open-source, or put in a portfolio.

The idea

01 · Essence
Brand essence
Give the things you grow a quiet voice — and the care to answer it.
Positioning

A four-channel soil-monitoring and automatic-watering system for people who love both plants and the craft of building things. It reads the soil and speaks for the plant, waters before a plant struggles, predicts what's next — and surfaces it all through an interface that feels alive, not industrial.

One-liner

“The smart, kind way to keep four plants perfectly watered.”

For

Plant-loving makers, home-automation tinkerers, and indoor gardeners — plus anyone reviewing the work in a portfolio or repo.

Attributes
Attentive Warm Alive Plain-spoken Crafted Calm

The name

02 · Naming

“Sprout” stays as a placeholder — the whole identity is name-agnostic, so the seedling mark and system survive a rename untouched. When you're ready to claim something ownable, here are three territories and a shortlist.

Soil & root

Grounded, crafted, a little obscure — great for an open-source maker tool. Depth and foundation.

Tend & reach

Built on the verb at the product's heart — caring for, reaching toward. Warm and active.

Measure & moisture

Where nature meets the instrument — water, dew, readings. Techy but still organic.

Shortlist — check availability before committing

TendrilTend & reachThe climbing shoot that reaches out — and hides “tend” right inside it, echoing your “tend well” voice. Friendly, evocative, ownable.top pick
TilthSoil & rootThe crumbly health of well-worked soil. Short, rare, premium; reads as something a careful grower would know.premium
LoamSoil & rootRich, fertile earth. Four letters, calm and warm; easy to say, easy to draw a mark around.
DewpointMeasureA real moisture-measurement term that sounds like nature. Bridges the “precise instrument” and “living thing” halves of the brand.
SprigTend & reachA small shoot — keeps Sprout's friendly feel and first letters, but is its own word. The gentle, safe rename.
Recommendation: Tendril for warmth + the “tend” connection, or Tilth if you want quiet, premium and rare. The mark and palette below work for any of them.

Voice & tone

05 · Personality

The brand speaks the way the plants do: calm, attentive, a little fond — the quiet friend who notices the soil so you don't have to. State the fact, then the feeling. One short line.

Data leads, warmth follows

Always earn the feeling with a fact. “29% · ready for water now,” not “I'm soooo thirsty!”

Fond, never fawning

A quiet companion. Affection shows through attention and restraint, not exclamation marks.

Short, then silent

One line is plenty. If there's nothing useful to say, the plant says nothing.

One voice, many rooms

Imagery & messaging

06 · Going to market

The seedling mood set is the brand's signature device — the same glyphs that live in the product carry the marketing. Around them: warm, real, hands-on imagery, never sterile stock.

Signature device · the moods

Use them as a row, a single hero glyph, a loading state, sticker art, even a favicon set. They are the most recognizable, most ownable thing the brand has — lean on them.

Photography direction
  • Real plants in natural side-light — windowsills, not studios.
  • Hands tending; macro soil, water droplets, a probe in the dirt.
  • Show the real hardware — relays, tubing, wires as craft.
  • Warm, slightly desaturated; soil-dark & cream backdrops.
  • Avoid glossy stock, fake gradients, perfect symmetry.
