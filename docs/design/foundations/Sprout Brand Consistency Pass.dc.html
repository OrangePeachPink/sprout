SPROUT · BRAND CONSISTENCY PASS

One Sprout, everywhere.

A full alignment pass across all eighteen design pages and the GitHub-facing surfaces — checking that every page, color, component, and line speaks in the one voice the brand evolved into: modern, warm, and plant-forward, with Sprout center stage.

This page is both the record of what changed and the reference for how to keep it consistent. The reasoning behind the not-taken routes is distilled in _archive/roads-not-taken.md; every pre-alignment original is recoverable from Git history.

The one standard
The canon every surface is measured against. When in doubt, this wins.
VOICE
Write as Sprout — first person, on plant-facing surfaces. Fact, then feeling. One short, glanceable line. Calm under fault. No baby-talk, no emoji, no guilt, no invented numbers. Tooling & process copy stays plain and warm — not first person.
THE LIVING MARK
One mark — two leaves on a stem. Static when it must be, alive when it can. Sway (idle) · breathe (thriving) · droop (thirsty) · bob (greeting). Honors reduced-motion.
COLOR & SOIL MODE
Consume tokens, never redefine. Leaf #34A853, the seven --band-* meaning colors. Soil (dark) mode is the refined cool green-charcoal — never the old warm-olive.
TYPE
Baloo 2 for display & Sprout's voice · Hanken Grotesk for UI & body · JetBrains Mono for every number and datum, always.
HONESTY & THE BOUNDARY
Raw + band are the reading; any 0–100 is a labelled relative index, never VWC. Mood follows the band, never the index. Character layers beside the instrument — never inside the numbers.
THE SIGN-OFF
tend well.

What changed — soil mode, brought to v3

The biggest drift was the dark theme. The v2-era pages still used the original warm-olive soil mode and the old acid green; v3 / ADR-0008 refined those neutrals to a cool green-charcoal and softened the green, but the early pages predated it. This pass folds the refinement into every live page so soil mode reads the same everywhere.

How to apply this — the short version
If you're making or touching a Sprout surface, this is the checklist that keeps it one voice:
→Speak as the plant on plant-facing copy; plain & warm everywhere else.
→Pull colors from the tokens; for dark, use the cool green-charcoal set.
→Let the mark be alive; pick motion by mood; honor reduced-motion.
→Numbers are mono and honest; mood follows the band, not the index.
→Keep character beside the instrument, never inside the readouts.
→Close with tend well. — and no emoji in product copy.
