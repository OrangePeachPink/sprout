The chip says what the band says.

Sprout's mood is a 1:1 function of the seven calibrated bands — never invented, never dialled for drama (ADR-0007 §5, ADR-0004). The source of truth is mood-band-map.json. Design-QA found the dashboard's card chips wording 3 of the 7 bands off-map, all toward alarm. This rules the canonical words so Data can fix to spec.

Why these three

“Drowning,” “Soggy” and “Critical” each swap Sprout's calm, fond register for alarm. The map's words — soaked · refreshed · faint — state the fact and let the plant keep its dignity. Faint matters most: air-dry is ambiguous (“in soil, or slipped out?”), so the word names a feeling, never declares death.

The standing rule

A chip word is read from the band, never authored per surface. One band, one mood word, from mood-band-map.json. New copy ideas go to the map first; if it isn't in the map, it isn't a chip.

for Data
Retire the three drama words in the card-chip map: Drowning→soaked, Soggy→refreshed, Critical→faint. Bind chips to mood-band-map.json as the single source so they can't drift again. Voice lines already track these moods in voice-strings.json — no change needed there.
