SPROUT · BRAND GUIDELINES
v1 · reference · ADR-0007

The brand is a plant that finally has a voice.

Sprout is a living, animated character — the hero of every surface. It speaks in the first person, narrates its own day, and asks nothing of you but the occasional refill. Everything else — color, type, data, the apps — is the soil it grows from.

living character first-person voice honest by default

01 · The direction we chose

Plant's POV — it speaks for itself

Five directions were explored. One won; the rest were excluded for a reason worth keeping on record — so we don't drift back into them.

CHOSENPlant's POV
The plant narrates everything in its own first-person voice. Charming, warm, alive — and it puts the plant, not the engineering, at the center.
EXCLUDEDField Journal
A naturalist's serif almanac — beautiful as an artifact, but it sidelines the live character and reads as something to study, not someone to meet.
EXCLUDEDBeautiful Instrument
Centers the engineering, not the plant — the default we're escaping. Its rigor survives as the honesty thread inside the winner, not the whole identity.
EXCLUDEDRetro-Futurism · Tamagotchi
A decorative skin without a reason, and a needy pet that wants you — both wrong for an ambient, hands-off system that asks nothing of you.

03 · The mood system

mood follows the band, never the index
INVARIANT
Sprout's mood is a 1:1 function of the calibrated moisture band — one of seven — and never of the 0–100 relative index. This is what lets personality and data-honesty coexist: the character can never contradict the instrument, because it reads from the same source of truth (ADR-0004).

Two honesty notes. The air-dry band is also the diagnostic “probe may not be in soil” band — so its voice names the ambiguity rather than dramatizing death. And “asleep” (the night / diurnal rest state) is a time overlay, not a moisture band — it can ride on top of any mood after dark.

04 · The voice

first person, always

We stopped writing about the plant and started writing as it. Every surface speaks in Sprout's first-person voice — calm, fond, honest. The fact leads; the warmth follows.

DO
→ Speak in the first person — “I”, never “your plant”.
→ Fact, then feeling — the data earns the warmth.
→ One short line. Glanceable. Never a paragraph.
→ Stay calm under fault — reassure, explain, pause.
DON'T
✕ No baby-talk or cutesy mangling of words.
✕ No emoji — the mark and color carry the feeling.
✕ No guilt-tripping — Sprout never nags or shames.
✕ No fake numbers — never invent a % or an ETA.
VOICE BY SURFACE
IN-APP
“I'm thriving. Last drink was two days ago.”
PUSH
“Getting thirsty over here — no rush, maybe tomorrow.”
FAULT
“I can't feel my soil sensor — I've paused my pump to be safe.”
SOCIAL
“Day 30: still alive, still green. We're a good team.”
README
“Hi, I'm Sprout. Here's how I keep four plants happy.”
EMPTY STATE
“No plants yet — add one and I'll start watching.”

06 · Character meets instrument

the boundary

The single most important rule for everyone building on Sprout: character layers onto the instrument — it never restyles it. Sprout sits beside the data and reinforces it; the dense numbers stay clean, mono, and honest.

SPROUT BELONGS HERE
“I'm thriving today.”
RAW · BAND
1,352 Moist
Ambient, empty, loading, onboarding, notifications, the single-plant hero — Sprout beside the gauge, both reading the same band.
