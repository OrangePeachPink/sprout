Flash Sprout 🌱

Get Sprout's firmware onto your ESP32 straight from this page — plug in USB, click Install. No Arduino IDE, no toolchain, no command line.

This browser can't flash a board. You need Chrome or Edge on a desktop or laptop — Safari, Firefox, and phones/tablets don't have the Web Serial API. Open this page there and you're set. You'll need to allow the serial port when the browser asks. Click Install again and pick your board from the list.

What you need
  1. A desktop or laptop (Windows, macOS, or Linux).
  2. Chrome or Edge — any Chromium browser. Not Safari, Firefox, or a phone.
  3. A USB data cable (charge-only cables won't enumerate the board).

After this one-time flash, Sprout updates itself over Wi-Fi — the cable is a first-day thing, not a living dependency.

The two prompts you'll see
  1. Serial-port chooser — the browser asks which device to connect to. Pick the ESP32; it's usually labelled by its USB-serial chip ("CP2102 USB to UART Bridge", "Silicon Labs CP210x", or "USB-SERIAL CH340").
  2. "Erase device?" — say yes for a fresh board; it does a clean install.

No port in the list? The USB-serial driver may be missing (CH340 boards sometimes need the WCH driver on Windows). The full walkthrough is in FLASHING.md.
